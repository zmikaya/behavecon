Chance that Dam 1 fails

The chance that a dam fails depends on the number of conditions that are present. More specifically, Table 1 summarizes the chance that Dam 1 will fail based on whether one or more of conditions A, B, C, D, E, and F is currently present at Dam 1:

  • If all six conditions are present at Dam 1, Dam 1 will fail with a 50% chance.
  • If exactly five of the six conditions are present (for example, conditions A, B, C, D, and E are present but condition F is not), Dam 1 will fail with a 40% chance.
  • If exactly four of the six conditions are present (for example, conditions A, B, C, and D are present but conditions E and F are not), Dam 1 will fail with a 30% chance.
  • If exactly three of the six conditions are present (for example, conditions A, B, and C are present but conditions D, E and F are not), Dam 1 will fail with a 20% chance.
  • If exactly two of the six conditions are present (for example, conditions A and B are present but conditions C, D, E and F are not), Dam 1 will fail with a 15% chance.
  • If only one of the six conditions is present, Dam 1 will fail with a 10% chance.
  • If none of the conditions are present, Dam 1 will fail with a 5% chance.
